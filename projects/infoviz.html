Information Visualization of Crimes and Socio-Economic Conditions

This was a project in the course TNM048 - Information Visualisation held at Linköping University in 2012. In the course, the students gather large amounts of abstract data and try to find and showcase possible correlations and contexts. Together with two friends I chose to look closer at any correlation between socio-economic conditions and the amount of reported crimes in Sweden during a period of 10 years. The data was on a per-month and per-municipality basis, and was gathered from a number of government databases.

To visualize this vast amount of data, we used the GAV framework and wrote the application in C# .NET. I also wrote a custom component in DirectX used to study correlations more in-depth, seen in the top-right part of the image below.
